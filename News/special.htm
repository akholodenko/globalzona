Bring in the Big Boys
[09.26.00]
Chervolet has come out with a new version of the long-time popular Suburban SUV. The new edition is called the OnShore. The vehicle is full of the top of the line gear from bumper to bumper. Inside, the truck has been gutted, reupholstered, and rewired with enough infotainment gear to start your own Sharper Image store. The hub of the electronics is a retractable Alpine CVA 1005 stereo/nav/DVD control unit that also displays a rear-camera view when the vehicle is put in reverse. Flush-mounted LCD video screens are mounted in the front headrests, and the DVD player and the Sega Dreamcast are hidden in the center console and rear armrest, respectively. Remote controls and wireless headphones abound.
European Exotica
[07.23.00]
New automobiles are covering the streets these days. Some people might be regretting spending their money on expensive SUVs due to the latest gas price jumps, but hey, if you got the cash for the car, you got some for the gas as well.

Some of the latest gas-guzzlers include the last run of the famous Lamborghini Diablo, this time in VT6 form. The last version of the legendary series will boost up to a full 543-hp rage, featuring the typical V-12 engine. The six-digit price tagïed Italian vehicle crams 60 mph in a mind-blowing 4.3 seconds.

Another fossil-fuel eater the the prototype Chrysler - made Dodge MaxxCab. The vehicle is nowhere near production, but does feature a variety of off-the-hook specs. The truck packs the Next Generation 4.7-liter Magnum V-8 with 238 horsepower. In addition, Dodge claims a 0-60 time of 7.7 seconds.

Last, not least, and closest to production and affordability is the 2001 Ford Escape XLT. As another addition to the popular brand of Ford SUVs (Explorer, Excursion, Expedition), the Escape will be equipped with the more powerful of the two 3.0-liter V-6s found in the Taurus: the 200-hp, 24-valve Duratec.

While high Vs in engine specs are popular now, in a few years the trend might completely disappear. Who knows where the world of car design will take us.
