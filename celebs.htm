Back for Another Five!
[12.18.00]
Okay, Stern fanatics, you can breathe now. Howard Stern's not hanging up his microphone--at least not yet. Just days before his current contract was set to expire, the radio shock jock signed a deal with Infinity Broadcasting that will keep his ranting self on the airwaves for at least another five years. Long-time listeners were aghast at the notion that the self-proclaimed "King of all Media" might put his nationally syndicated talk show to pasture, especially with Howard repeatedly stating on-air that Friday's show could be his last. While some suggested Stern was just trying to scare an extra few dollars out of the powers-that-be, Howard insisted his decision to re-up was tough. "I was really on the fence," Stern tells the Associated Press. "But in the end, I couldn't see walking away from this."
Leo Under Fire
[12.12.00]
A late night street fight involving the reigning king of the world has quickly mushroomed into a courtroom battle of titanic proportions, as a Manhattan appeals court has ruled a $45 million lawsuit brought against him can officially go to trial. And that means fans can see Leo for free during the court proceedings, which will likely go down next year. Roger Wilson, a 41-year-old screenwriter and B-movie star of Porky's, filed a lawsuit against DiCaprio for allegedly inciting his entourage to attack Wilson in a scuffle over Showgirls actress Elizabeth Berkley .
Madonna is Robbed
[12.06.00]
On November 30th, the London estate of the pop super star was burglarized. In addition to minor items, the thieves were unfortunately able to get their hands on Madonna's fiancée's [Guy Ritchie] Range Rover. The couple, along with Madonna's two children were asleep upstairs when the crime took place. The car has been recovered, but no arrests made. The singer purchased the $10 million mansion within the last few weeks, moving to London for safety reasons.
