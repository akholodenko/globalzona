Girl, Interrupted
by
Artem Kholodenko
Girl, Interrupted Review This film happened to be equally as crazy as its characters. The basic plot was about a girl with some emotional problems being sent to a mental institution and discovering new things about life and people. Although you learn to look past the obvious about people and search for their inner self, you are left with a head full of strange scenes at the end of the film. As many things in the world are, this is a picture that would probably appeal to people of certain likes, yet would be a "thumb down" for those with different enjoyments. For the deep and intellectual types, who enjoy a good challenge in figuring out the meaning behind a movie, "Girl, Interrupted" is perfect. However for the rest of us a simpler and less brain-bending production would have been more accepted. One of the interesting factors is that the actors really did a good job on their parts. It was whether that or they were really mentally unstable. Their problems are believable and realistic and emphasize the setting even more. Over the entire movie gets a B because disregarding the negative aspects, the positive ones still scored some points.
