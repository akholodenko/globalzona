3OHA ADK Rating: B+
Rating Legend
-
"Here we go again!" was probably a phrase, which many mumbled when it came to the trailer of "Frequency". Appearing to be just another sci-fi time rippling flick, the movie didn't bring much uniqueness to the screen; luckily, it proved us wrong in the theater.
The basic plot of "Frequency" involves a father in '69 and his grown up son in '99 being able to communicate through a ham radio due to temporary magnetic fields in earth's atmosphere. As always, when it comes to time, one new event or move can have long lasting and drastic effects, as happened here. When saving his dad from death, the '99 version of the son caused a change in the space - time - continuum and allowing a killer (not his dad) to live and kill an additional 7 women, including his mother. Now the time - cracking duo must use their cop/firefighter skills to prevent the disasters from actually occurring.
The film is one of the few that can actually be compared with the suspense of the Oscar - nominated "Six Sense". The unpredictable sequences and events lead to closing usually present loopholes. Sitting in the theater seat, one doesn't wonder what's going to happen (of course he does, but not as much as…), but instead, how it's going to happen; that is the basic idea of a sci-fi / mystery. The movie gave it's best try and it succeeded to an almost perfect score. Of course some minor improvements would have been possible, such as a bit more twisting of the mind with the murder cases. In the first scenes of the movie, news about a murderer are announced over a car radio. May be I've seen too many movies, or I just am good, but I predicted that, that is exactly what the film is going to be surrounding - not that fun to be able to tell form the first few seconds of the picture. Otherwise it was great and I would recommend to anyone who wants to get a real rush to go and see it!
