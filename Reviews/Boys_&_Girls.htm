BOYS & GIRLS

Synopsis
Freddie Prinze Jr. plays opposite Claire Forlani in this romantic comedy about two longtime friends who go to college at Berkeley. After they both suffer a string of failed romances, they start to think maybe they should be dating.




Review
As hundreds of pre-pubecent girls flack to the theaters to see the latest Freddie Prinze Jr. flick critics wonder how long the young mega - star can keep up the interest of the audience with his teen love romance movies. By the apparently loaded theaters - for some time.

Unfortunately, some think that Freddie's peak came at "She's All That", when he debuted as the lover on the scene, but let's talk about what "Boys & Girls" has in store for us.

While changing the style a bit from a "lovers" film to start with, Ryan (Freddie) and Jennifer (Clare Forlani) meet on a plane and run into each throughout high school and later college. They become close friends and as usually known, that leads to more, but as always, there's a problem.

I don't know about how hard it might be to act, but I think that after doing a number of these types of movies, Freddie should have it down better than that. On the other hand, his co-stars Clare Forlani and Jason Biggs kept the audience from leaving the theater. Biggs, who plays Hunter (Steve) Ryan's roommate and local "pimp" constantly brings on the jokes, making this film a real comedy. Forlani, who plays Ryan's love interest, depicts the dramatic aspect of the film, balancing Biggs's goofiness.

One thing is left to ponder: How many more of these films?
Movie Info.
Category: Romance Comedy
Starring: Freddie Prinze Jr., Clare Forlani and Jason Biggs
