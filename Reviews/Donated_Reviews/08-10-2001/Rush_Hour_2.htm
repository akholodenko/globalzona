RUSH HOUR 2

Synopsis
In case you didn't get enough the first time around, Jackie Chan and Chris Tucker are back in this sequel to the 1998 blockbuster Rush Hour. As seen at the end of the first movie, Detective James Carter travels back to Hong Kong with his new friend, Detective Lee, to fight Chinese crooks.



Review
        When you mix up two extremes such as Jackie Chan and Chris Tucker all that could come out of it is a soup of humor and action. In this sequel to the surprise runaway hit of 1998, the now-known characters find themselves in situations more humorous and unbelievable than ever before.
       Although it seems that the two are barely acting and are simply being their usual laugh/punch combo, the character of Lee (Jackie Chan) seems to become more Americanized in his ideas. While a sequel, the film seems more like a mirror image of the original. Due to the fact that the first half of the movie takes place in Hong Kong - in other words on Lee's turf, the character of Chris Tucker is the one that has to adjust to his environment, unlike the original picture. Many key phrases are now said by Tucker's Asia counterpart.
       Yet the film is not all laughter and action. Certain elements of the plot to present themselves as intriguing and thought-provoking. Surprisingly there is even a plot twist involved that is not too easily predicted.
       As rarely as this is said, it must be toward Rush Hour 2. The film is an excellent sequel that equals if not surpasses the original in a number of the key entertainment aspects. From what it seems, definitely expect a third installment of what could be Chris Tucker's ultimate claim to fame. (We all know that Jackie Chan has already made a big enough name for himself not to need additional claim to fame.)
Movie Info.
Category: Action Comedy
Starring:Jackie Chan, Chris Tucker, Zhang Zi Yi, Don Cheadle
Directed by: Brett Ratner
Rated: PG-13
Running Time : 88
Rating: A
