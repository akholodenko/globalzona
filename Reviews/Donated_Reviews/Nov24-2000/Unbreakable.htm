UNBREAKABLE

Synopsis
Director M. Night Shyamalan reunites with his Sixth Sense star Bruce Willis in this supernatural thriller about a security guard who is the sole survivor of a devastating train wreck. Samuel L. Jackson plays a crippled stranger who helps Willis understand his miraculous ability to survive all disasters, thus making him "unbreakable."



Review
       "Unbreakable" is definitely a film that provoked a varying list of reactions. However, there were two groups that emerged miraculously as the winning parties: the people who were totally for the film and the people who were totally against the film.
        One thing that was absolutely obvious was the director's attempts to recapture that feeling that "The Sixth Sense" brought to the screen. However, while with excellent strides for success, this film did not make it to the ranks of its predecessor.
        It seemed that the introduction of the lengthy feature took up most of the screen time, leaving only few precious minutes to a brief explanation that did not capture the hearts of audiences and surprise as many as the ghostly ancestor did.
        While a brave attempt, "Unbreakable" only partially succeeded in producing the dramatic effect for which it was intended to. However, without attempts, there would be no successes.
Movie Info.
Category: Thriller
Starring: Bruce Willis, Samuel L. Jackson and Robin Wright Penn
Directed by: M. Night Shyamalan
Rated: PG-13
Running time: 117 min.
Rating: D+
