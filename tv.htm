TV: 2000 Annual Emmy Awards 05.22.00
This year's Emmy Award Show was televised last Saturday, the 20-th of May. As always, it was exciting and heart-breaking, the show brought in a large audience to observe the acceptance speeches of such greats as the writing team of The Young and the Restless. Additional winners included Bob Barker of The Price is Right, who tied for the award of "Best Game Show Host" with Tom Bergeron of Hollywood Squares.
TV: Prime-Time Shows on the Move 05.17.00
The NBC network has announced the the television programming of the prime-time shows. Some of the major changes including the movement of the hit show Frasier to Tuesday nights, from its previous position on Thursday nights. Additional changes include the move of another hit, Will & Grace from their old spot on Tuesdays to a new one on Thursdays. Friends will keeps its current spot.
TV: Friends Settle Contact Difference 05.15.00
The cast of NBC's hit television series, Friends has figured out its contacts for the next two years. David Schwimmer, Lisa Kudrow, Matthew Perry, Jennifer Aniston, Courteney Cox Arquette and Matt LeBlanc will each receive $750,000 per episode, while their share of syndication rights will double from a half point to a full point, according to industry sources.

TV: Doctors Getting RichER 05.06.00
With the conclusion of this television season, Friends is not the only show talking about new contract plans. The hit hospital drama ER is also in the middle of finishing up their dealings with contracts. From the plans so far, the show will be extended at least until 2004, but that's not it. Stars Anthony Edwards, Noah Wyle and Eriq LaSalle have contracts (between $27 and $35 million each) only through 2002, and if they're like fellow residents Julianna Margulies, Gloria Reuben, Kelly Martin and, of course, George Clooney, they could hang up their scrubs for good when the contracts expire.
