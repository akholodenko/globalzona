Journal Entry
07.2.00
Sunday is unfortunately coming to an end and I can honestly say that the weekend was well spent. Let us start with Saturday.

Yesterday, as I previously said, I went out to eat with my girlfriend. We went to "The Stinking Rose". It's a restaurant that serves all of its food with garlic. It was really fun. And I really have to give it up to them for the speed of the service. Like two seconds after the waitress took our order, the salad and soup came. Right after we finished that, the main course followed. The meal was followed for desert at rapid speeds, only to be concluded with a semi-extensive wait for the bill. So all in all, we spend no more than an hour 1/2 in the place.

After that we went down to walk around the pleasantly crowded North Beach district of San Francisco. The place - unlike the district I live in - actually had a lot of people out and about the streets, with excitement buzzing all around.

Later in the evening we went to see "Boys & Girls". I saw the movie before, but my girlfriend wanted to see and I was looking forward to it again. For some reason it was a lot better this time around, and I enjoyed it a lot more.
Today, finally my cousin's plan came true. She had been talking about having this whole young family day out. Where all of the cousins and brothers and sisters in our generation would go out with their significant others to do something. We don't usually see each other often, and when we do, it's not often we are all there at the same time. So it sounded like a great idea.

After some putting off due to schedule interference, we finally figured out that today was the day. We went to "Malibu Grand Prix". It's a place 30 minutes away from San Francisco, which has mini-indy racing, arcades, batting cages, and mini-golf. We went for the mini-golf, although we decided to come back for the cars as well.

A few hours down, we finished up our rounds and with our sunburned faced forward, headed off to get something to eat. We went all - you - can - eat Japanese style. It was damn good stuff. After that we parted, with sure plans to repeat the family experience.
