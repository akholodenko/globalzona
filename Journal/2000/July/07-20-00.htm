Journal Entry
07.20.00
The count down has begun. Today is Thursday and I am leaving for Israel on Saturday. I actually am looking toward the trip because I am going to see some relatives, which I haven't seen for years.

I have organized a bunch of the stuff that I am going to take with already and more to come. The bad thing is that it is very hot there and I hope I can take the heat.

Unfortunately, the web site will not be updated for the time that I am gone [07.22.2000-08.08.2000] and I will update it when I come back. I also hope that I don't miss anything interesting in the USA while I am gone.

Otherwise, it is all good. Going over to my girlfriend's house later today to chill. We were going to join some other people in seeing X-Men: The Movie, but that feel through, so I won't be able to see it until I come back. May be there will be a theater in Israel. Who knows.

I am also hoping to run into a cyber cafe in Israel, however, I think I will be out of luck. Unfortunately, no one I know there has Internet access because it is very pricey. In Israel, the second you pick up the phone receiver, the company starts charging you. So it is not good to leave the phone off the hook.
Have you heard about the world of online banking. I have and let me tell you about it. The latest place is called NetBank.com and it is a virtual bank. They only have one location in Georgia, diverging the money saved into higher interest rates. You could pay off bill online and get checks and a Visa check card. Deposits are sent in to the place and trust me, they get there. From San Francisco it takes about five days.

Since the place has no ATMs around, they allow you to use other banks' without charging you for it. However, many other banks have their own fees (usually $1.50). So a list of totally free ATMs is available. These banks include Patelco and Bayview Bank.
June 2000
