Journal Entry
06.09.00
Another day went by and I didn't even notice. It's really weird with time these days. I was taking a walk through the park and was thinking about a time when some friends and I went out, and I recalling it the stuff. It seemed a while ago, but not that long ago Then I thought for a second and realized that it was about a year ago. Where does the time go?

Yesterday I had some fun. I was talking to a friend of mine and she invited my girlfriend and I to go fishing with some people in the evening. I knew we wouldn't catch any fish, but thought it would still be fun. So we went and after getting set up the equipment. About an hour or more after freezing our asses off we decided that our luck with fish was gone for the night and decided to call it quits. Instead, we headed off to get something warm to eat and drink and to beddie-bye to sleep. It was a lot of fun and I can't wait to freeze gain.

I just love wearing three or four layers of warm clothing and still being a little cold. In San Francisco you just don't get to do that kind of thing very often, and I love every chance I get.
Today I went to my girlfriend's house to continue the bike riding lessons I began to give her. She;s going away to college and wanted to learn how to ride a bike to get around the campus.

I rode my mom's purple girl's bike across Golden Gate Park looking as cool as ever and we started to practice again. It was progressive and she just needs to gain more confidence.

I love spending time with her because I feel so relaxed and that there are no worries. Good stuff. In addition, the weather sparks a thought of goodness in the heart. Today was exceptionally sunny in SF, unlike yesterday's foggy and drizzly weather. These are the days that I feel I am truly in Sunny California and not in London.
Tomorrow I am planning to go watch "Gone in 60 Seconds", staring Nicholas Cage and Angelina Jolie. The film is about how Cage's character is put in a situation where he has to steal 50 cars in 24 hours to save his brother's life. In order to achieve that goal, he hires a group of professional car thieves to help his out. The film is fast - pased and keeps you on the edge of your toes. A review is sure to follow.
June 2000
