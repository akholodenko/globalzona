Journal Entry
06.12.00
I am a working man! I am kind of tired right now. I worked today for over 5 1/2 hours. It might not be much, but I did it without a break.

My day started off kind of early. I had to go to school for a second to pick up my summer school schedule. As I said before I am going to be taking American Democracy. Luckily, my class was scheduled for the first period, so I will be at school from 8am to 10:15pm. It's a lot of time, but since we have to cover a full semester in a five - week time period, I understand.

So when I got there, I was not informed of the time I was suppose to pick it up, or when my last name would be called or anything. After running into a friend of mine, I went to see what what up and found out that while I was there at 8:30am already, my last name (K) would not be up until 10am. Great, and I had to get to work hopefully by 10am. A really nice guy was calling people into the cafeteria to get their schedule, and after informing him of my situation, I was allowed to go in right then and there. Within five minutes I had my schedule and was ready to go out, when I saw my friend again, who actually got screwed because her schedule was messed up. I would have stuck around, but unfortunately I had to bust a move over to work.

Seeing that I was going to be extremely early, I didn't bother speeding through the street and simply took my time. Even when I did, I still ended up coming to work a sweet half an hour early.

The day wasn't much after I got home, mostly computer, phone, and the TV. I kind of bounce between those three when I'm home and have nothing to do. I am happy about how work is turning out to be, with me actually working real hours.
Currently I am reading an action-thriller novel called The Grid by Philip Kerr. It's a modern - day sci-fi story about an architectural firm, which is ending the work on a new building dubbed the "Gridiron". The building is hi-tech, with the computer, Abraham, controlling all of the systems, including life-support, security, and nearly everything else. Suddenly, dead bodies begin to show up through out the building and at the climax, the employees get trapped, with no way out but death.

The story is great and is keeps you reading for more. I've only had the 400+ - page book for a couple of days, but it pulled me in to much, I am almost done with it.
June 2000
