Journal Entry
06.16-17-18.00
Hey, sorry I have not written anything for a couple of days, but I was a bit out of it. I was putting it off until I had something to write, but then when I did I was too tired and couldn't do anything.

Lets see, on Friday 16th I had a day off and spend most of it cooped up at home. In the evening, a couple of my friends and I went to see Freddie Prinze Jr.'s new film, "Boys & Girl". I'm not going to go into it now, but you can read all about it in the review I wrote. Plus, if you are interested in all of the other movies that came out, check out Global Zona's movie section.

We didn't do anything afterward due to personal reasons of one of the accompaniments, and I was glad that it was all over because I really wasn't having much fun.
Following that day, yesterday, the 17th was better. One of the friends from the movies came over to my house and we went for a walk at Ocean Beach. After dropping by the Safeway supermarket, we went to a ledge at the Ocean Board walk to eat the newly purchased ice cream. That half a pint sure went down well - not!

Anyway, we walked around Golden Gate Park some more before cruising back to my place to chill for a while. We didn't do much at my house and my friend later went home.
Today I have been invited to my cousin's birthday picnic. He's turning 23. I invited my girlfriend to come along with me, but unfortunately she is busy. That really sucks, cause I know I would have a lot more fun with her being there.

Switching gears and turning to cars. You, some think that the former Soviet Union is a lost hope for democracy and economical flourishment. Well I have something to prove them wrong. Since the end of the communist era in 1991, the car production has been increasing and new competitive European models have been forming. To check out some of the Russian cars, click here.

It's really cool to see how all of Europe is comings up to the same standards. And the Russian cars are not just coming from one brand. There is actually competition going like from this company here.
I'm back home now. Where from you ask? Well if you would have read the sentences of this journal entry above you would know that I went to my cousin's birthday picnic. It was cool. Ate some food, played some cards. Overall better than sitting at home.

My younger cousin (birthday guy's little sister) suggested a cool thing: for all of us cousins, including my sister and all and our wives/husbands/girlfriends/boyfriends to go chill somewhere and just hang out. That's a totally great idea and we will probably be doing that during the Independence Day long weekend. The idea that has been pitched is to go play miniature golf or some type of interactive activity.
June 2000
