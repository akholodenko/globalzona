Journal Entry
06.03.00
Unfortunately, the end of the school year, does not mean the end of worries for an average high junior. What takes over is the fear and anxiety of SATs, both I and II. My SAT II exam is this morning at some school that is not even in my city, if not county. I guess I can only plame myself for registering in the last minute, but who cares, I'm not walking there, now am I.

I've been studying for the exam for this past week for about 2 hours a day, give or take - mostly give. Hey, although it's not a fun thing to do, I feel better after I have done it and have nothing to blame myself for when the scores come in the mail.

This school year I sure did learn how to study, all thanks to my math teacher. After flunking the first exam due to lack of readyness (if that is the truth, then over half the class wasn't ready), I decided it was time to change the approach I take toward school work and exams. After studying for a quota for two hours for an exam, I noticed significant improvements in my scoring and was glad that I spent the time self - educating.

Anyway, I will be getting back with more information regarding the SAT II exam, including all of the hints and suggestions, and more. However, I can give you some suggestions now:

• Start studying at least two weeks (three - four weeks) before.

• Don't stress out and relax.

• Take your time and it's better to do five and get them right, than do ten and get three right.
Am I happy that the SAT II exam is finally over. While the test was not as pleasant as desired to take, the environment was totally enjoyable. The San Bruno school I took the exam at was one of those stereotypical all-american schools with a cool design. The hallways were lit with daylight, which came down through the transparent roof and the classrooms were small and clean.

But let's get to the important aspect: the test. I took three sections: Writing, Math IC, and US History. The writing included a stupid essay and a bunch of multiple choice questions for which is not hurrying, one would not have had time for. The math was pretty easy, because I practiced a lot at home. The history was surprisingly weird: 90 questions in 60 minutes. Now for the tips:

• Take your time on questions, but don't take too much of it.

• If you are totally blank on an answer, skip it; if down to two or three, guess.

• If you're not good at memorizing math formulas, make sure you can think logically around the problem.
