The 2000 Academy Awards were a site to behold! With the bleachers full of start-struck fans, the celebrities strolled down the red carpet on Sunday, March 26, 2000. Dressed up in fancy gowns and stylish tuxedos, the audience poured in on stretch limos, which crowed the L.A. freeways for hours. As the curtain rose and Billy Crystal showed his face, everyone knew that this evening would bring unforgettable memories.
The Oscars were a little bit different this year. From recollections, the typical films, which won were dramatic tales of love and lust, such as "The English Patient" and "Braveheart"; however, this year it was different. Films like "American Beauty" and "The Sixth Sense", which took the record for having the youngest supporting actor nominee that sense of conservatives, went away and a new environment took over, with young Hollywood rivaling the old.
Unfortunately, some downfalls came as well. Did any of you notice how one of the N*SYNC boys tripped on the staircase? Anyway, it is always enjoying watching a dedication piece made up of various film clippings - but only one! This time around, the Academy made sure to over do it, with four or five different sceneries put together. The effect and drama was totally lost and boredom took over. That is one aspect of the Oscars that I didn't expect. I fan of celebrities and the awards, I usually watch them with excitement, but this time around, I watch about half it the show, simply because I was too dull and boring.
Now we only have to wait to next year to roll around to see what films of today and tomorrow will make it to the big show and get the ageless golden guy. Just remember, just because a film won an Oscar, it doesn't mean that it's good - ah, whom am I kidding, and it is!
