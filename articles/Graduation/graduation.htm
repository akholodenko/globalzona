Something that almost all of us experience in life is high school graduation. The event is long awaited and short-lived. After twelve years of schooling, we are all eager to get out of the walls we called home for four years. Going into the "real world", we don't realize its hardships and troublesome times; luckily there are at least four years of college in the way of real life to give us a chance to get use to the new situation.
Some of the most stressful times one experience is during their senior year. Although it is said that junior year of high school is tough, senior is even worse. Besides the grueling assignments, there are the tedious and strenuous college applications. Not only does they take up time and money, you have to wait for the replies to come - in three month! People begin to get gray hairs over the stupid applications. In the end, some stop carrying about the stress, but only the results, while others see that the stress lead to more of it: finding a junior college nearby.
What does really happen to people after graduation? Let's think about this for a moment, shall we. There are a few ways to go after high school: college/university, travel, or work. Take higher for example: you go to school for twelve years and then you have to go for at least for four years more. What is the fun in that? You don't even get to enjoy your adult life except for the brief summer.
Travel might be the way to go. I mean you get out of high school and go all over the world finding more about others and yourself. Sounds like fun, but is it? You come back later - broke - and end up behind everyone who you use to be on the same level on. Interesting?! Maybe that vacation wasn't that much worth it.
Well, the last route is one that you would be taking out of necessity and not desire. If you needed money and have no other way out - I understand. However, if you slacked off in school the whole time and smoke weed instead of studying you deserve what you get (…at McDonald's today…). Why should someone who didn't do the work get the same rights and opportunities as a person who put in a lot of time and work to achieve their goals? They don't and they won't. No matter how lucky an individual might be for a short time luck runs out and you end up in the line of life; your place is where you deserve it.
