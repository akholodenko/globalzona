2000: Year in Review

        It went by in a flash of blood and fury. You didn't even look back to see what really happened, but a lot of things truly did.
        From it's disastrous beginning in the stock market, to the concluding US elections, Y2K proved to be anything but ordinary. As millions lost money due to a dramatic collapse of the tech sector in the NASDAQ, Microsoft Chairman Bill Gates took to court to disprove the monopolistic accusations against the company that he built from ground up. While still undecided, steps were taken against the guilty party into breaking the multi-billion corporation into smaller fractions on the technological front.
        While legal battles raged in the west, Europe didn't keep quiet as well. Events such as historic elections of Vladirim Putin as the president of the Russian Federation marked a historical spot for the year. In addition, terror raged through that continent with the outbreak of the so unpopular mad-cow decease. However, while Russia was looking toward a prosperous new time with a new leader, the Kursk submarine disaster in the cold waters of the Arctic brought the country under the magnifying glass of the world.
        The US continued the year with ongoing legal disputes, including the infamous Napster cases. The world of MP3's was being moved from secret warez web sites to your desktop with the use of that company's software. The freeware producer was led by a 19-teen year old president, who simply wanted to make the Internet a better place for all of us.
        The music world was continuously rocked by breaking milestones. The year was one to list a numerous list of broken sales records, including N*Sync's second album record sale debut at #1. Other show stoppers such as Britney Spears had her moments in the sun, including the announcement of her relationship with Justin Timberlake, of N*Sync.
        As the year began to wind down, the eye of the public shifted from the entertainment to the political arena. Post November news had repeating reports of Florida recounts and results as two worthy opponents battled for the prestigious right to lead one of the greatest world super powers into the new millennium. As now known, governor Bush of Texas won that election by the Democratic forfeit and is currently the president-elect.
        No one knows what 2001 will bring. Will it be new strange fashions or WWIII? The only way to find out is to bravely look the future in the eye and meet it half way. But one thing is for sure - get ready for the cyber world to be in every aspect of your life!
