Well, for those hardcore football fans out there, which don't get enough with the NFL - get ready for the XFL! The World Wrestling Federation announced the partner ship with NBC for a 50/50 control of the new league! The new league will return to the old football roots of the "real" game from the '60s and '70s. Due to the partnership with NBC, the league games will be broadcasted from February to April on Saturday night's prime time TV. This includes the broadcast of the XFL Championship game, first of which will be played on April 21, 2001.
Some new rules also come into play with the new league. Some of the changes made include eliminating the fair-catch rule (making punts live balls), requiring only one foot inbounds on pass receptions, instituting a 35-second play clock and limiting halftime to 10 minutes. In addition, spy cameras will be placed around the entire event, including player helmets, coaches, locker rooms, and benches.
Wives around the nation watch out! The additional sport means that not only do you loose Monday nights with your hubby, but now those precious Saturday nights are going to whither away as well! Word to the wise, take the batteries out of the remote and set the TV to the "LifeTime" channel. This way your husband will not be able to switch channels from his seat and as usual will be too lazy to get up and do the task manually. One way or another, the season begins play on February 3, 2001
