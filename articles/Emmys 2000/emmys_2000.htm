       After watching a simply five minutes of the 52nd Annual Emmy Award Show, one right away notices the absolute difference between the present production and the previous week's MTV Awards. The style and elegance of the Emmys doesn't compare with anything but the Oscars, which are a totally different story. As celebrities arrived on the famous red carpet, crowds of reporters and fans roared through the air. Passing from one interview station to another, stars melted in the 90° California sun. However, everyone hung tight, simply awaiting the moment they would enter the air-cooled auditorium. Inside were waiting for them golden statuettes, which "had more gold on them than in Snoop God's mouth," as one reported commented.
       The show began with a parody skit of last season's, NBC hit show, "Survivor". The tribe made of present and former comedians voted for who to elected to host the awards, with Garry Shandling winning in the end, simply because the people wanted him gone. He did start off with a few interesting facts about how he shaves one leg in bed to pretend he is with a woman.
       As it usually happens every year around, a certain handful of shows would wrack up most of the awards. The tradition this year started off with "Will & Grace" actors Megan Mullally and Sean Hayes grabbing the awards for the best supporting male/female actors in a comedy series.
       Luckily, some humor continued to show throughout the show, with a number of presenters joking about what it would have been like if homosexuality covered our screens from an earlier time period. A suggested TV show was, "Little Bathhouse on the Prarey".
       Among other winners were Halle Berry, who won the best actress award in a mini-series; Jack Lennon took the male part of that award. In addition, Michael J. Fox took the lovely golden lady home for his role as a comedic lead on the ABC show "Spin City". He was the favorite for the prize, due to his retirement because of his fight with the terminal illness he carries.
       As for the television shows, "The Coroner" won for the best mini-series, while "Saturday Night Live" for the best variety show, as "Oprah Winphrey Resents: Tuesdays with Morry" took the emmy for the best made for TV movie. "Will & Grace did once again shine as the best comedy series, while "The West Wing" took the award for the best drama series.
       The night was running past schedule, forcing presenters [who each got a gift basket worth $8,000, which included a Nokia cellular phone and a Palm Pilot to boot] to come out before called by the host, who was booted off stage so the show can wrap up..
