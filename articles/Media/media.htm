One source of the most bullshit and distortion is the world of news and information. At first these predators of knowledge seem like your best friend, trying to pull out any and all you might know about what they are interested it. Later, they turn on you, distort all of the information given and make you look like the ultimate super-villain.
Unfortunately, in our world, dirt sells and sells big! Tabloids are full of fallacious stories of alien babies and bisexual love affairs. Luckily most of us are sane enough to distinguish to reality from the BS of publishing. However, a negative article can ruin a person, especially with a reporter set on destroying their carrier. Even if the accusations are proven to be wrong, the incident - such as in some cases drugs, sex, and implants - will be associated with the individual or group for a long time.
For example, when Pee Wee Herman was caught performing "self-sexual" acts in an "adult" theater, the whole country knew it in a matter of hours. Although he didn't not deny his deed a lot of it was probably blow out of proportion. In another case dealing with the British pop singer, George Michaels, with him supposedly committing an "act" in a men's room. The celebrity did not deny the crime, but the publicity of it made his music less popular, since no one wanted to be associated with the singer.
The media could make you or break you; sometimes they do both. Watch out for them and you should be fine. The best thing to do is stay out of controversial situations; it's good to be in the news of neutral acts and events. However, one often wouldn't get in the news because of the lack of "juiciness" in the story.
