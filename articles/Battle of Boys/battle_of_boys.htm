It is interesting to observe the change in culture. Some find it ridiculous that at some point people actually loved and lived disco! They walked and walk and talked the talk. But 70s were just as pathetic and the 90s. By the close of the millennium, boy bands began popping up and covering music-store shelves with their albums. No matter the talent or message, if you were in a boy band, you made it big - it was like having a .COM.
Preying on the innocent and vulnerable teen girls, these vocal predators took the music industry by storm, bringing pop back to a point were some were actually proud of listening to it. The Vanilla Ice days were over - or so were thought.
Do you remember how cool it was to have that latest "New Kids on the Block" single? Well what do you think of it now? That is the same thing that is going to happen with all of these teen-heartthrobs, who make it big in a second and fall on their ass just as fast.
Yeah, they are great now. But wait until they get a little older, not as cute, and their voices change. I mean that has to happen some day - we all go through puberty, and the way performers are getting younger and younger, it seems like their carriers will be over before that memorable first zit. What are next - operatic babies being born singing like Pavarotti?
While being a big supporter of the boy-band craze, MTV went out a made a perfect spoof off the bands, with meaningless lyrics and cheesy moves. Their group, 2Gether is I think by far the best compilation of humor among five guys. Another parody were the Meaty Cheesy Boys of Jack in the Box. Used in their commercial to promote the greatest burger in the world, the Ultimate Cheese Burger, they sing about dreaming of the melting cheese. After checking out their website, I saw that the group is serious business and they go on tour in the fall.
Overall, this craze is complete non-sense. People should get more techno and dance things like Eiffel 65 to listen to. Unfortunately the conservative mood of the USA does not allow us to hear the great European entertainers, some of which are even bigger than Elvis!
