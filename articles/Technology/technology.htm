Technological Takeover
By
Artem Kholodenko
As evolution continues, humans become more dependent on their own inventions. Ages ago people only had simple and basic devices such as a hammer. However, as time went on, complex devices began to appear, like engines and other mechanical collaborations. By the end of the 20-th century people have become completely dependent on objects of their own creation. Ranging from home to work, humans can't escape the ever-expanding world of electronics.
Modern-day students rely on computers for school needs. There isn't an English teacher in USA that at one point or another requires his students to type-up an assignment. Yet, even today not all pupils have a PC at home; don't worry, the school have gotten plenty of funding from the "Cyber School" program to have some limited number of computer to student use. For those who might not have knowledge on how to operate one those complex machines, computer application classes are offered, while for those computer savvy folks, programming classes do the jobs.
The work place is another location where to some degree one has to deal with inventions. Whether it is automobiles, computers, copy machines, or phones, we all rely on man-made machines in a way. Now lets think about what would happen if everything suddenly broke and stopped working - chaos!
If technology seized to exist humanity would collapse, as we know it. The stock market would not be able to function and millions would be lost every second. Along with that, mobility would be impossible and the lack of communication of modern-day would cripple us even more. Overall, everything would be affected toward the negative, and Earth would enter a world of crime, violence, and mishap. So lets just make sure of one thing: we invented technology; don't let it reinvent you!
