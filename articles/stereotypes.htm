Parental Relations
By
Artem Kholodenko
Parental Relationships Teens: how many times have you been faced with a disagreement between yourselves and your parents for which you have an excellent argument, yet your point of view was still neglected? I know I've been there. It's tough for the youth to be taken seriously because the parents always have the upper hand and the final word. No matter how right a teen might be, the parents will never admit their mistake or wrong-doing; it's just part of being the parental task force.
Although older and wiser, the parents should treat their children with the same respect that they wish for their children to treat them. Yelling at off springs only causes another proof of Newton's 3rd law: for every action there is the same, yet opposite reaction. In other words, teens just yell back the same way. To get humane reactions to parental totalitarian control parents need to approach conversations with children in a diplomatic and calm way. This will cause surprising and respectful reactions from the younger generations.
From personal experience, when told to do something, I have a negative and forceful feeling to do the opposite and refuse. This is because I feel as though I am being made to do a certain task. If I was approached and asked to do the same task I would have a positive response and willingly complete the assignment.
Looking at schools from one point of view, the institutions are created to teach people how to obey orders. Students must listen to the instructions of the teachers and do what they are told. The punishment, although not a severe as at home, results in a poor grade, and the reward in excellent results. This pleases the parents, which in turn reward the children once again. In the old days, the schools, the system of desks, the definition of classrooms, the completing of assignments were all preparations for the real world and factories. The same principles applied to those institutions with working behind desks, among a collective, and in order to complete work.
The tables have in fact been turned on instructors, whether of the parental or teacher type. With the technological age at its peak, the average youth holds more cybernetic knowledge than an average adult. Yet adults, especially parents, think that they are superior to their children in some or every way. A relationship, no matter of what type, involves listening, understanding, and compromising. If any of the aspects are missing, a healthy relationship does not exist.
What is left to remember when the two powerhouse forces meet in a battle of words and raised voices is to keep cool and then everything will be fine. The second that a voice is raised, coolness is lost; along with which a clear and conscious thinking leaves, too. Parents and kids: chill!!!
