If you have ever been to Europe or even outside of the USA, you have probably noticed that there is a difference among many things, including clothes, culture, and more. Some however, probably thought of those differences as being worse than the US and as mere imperfections on the part of the locals.
On the contrary, US one of the most conservative countries in certain cases: one of these being music. European music shops are filled with albums of globally known artists, which are never heard of in the US. Great performers that didn't make to the US claim that the lack of open-mind individuals causes the lack of record sales. Once in a while a act breaks through, and as much as reports say that the group is one of a kind and brings a new talent to the industry, it's the same thing. Take Spice Girls for example. The group was British, wild, but still pop-like to appeal to the US ear. However, groups like Modern Talking, which has been selling albums by the million in Europe since 1984 and is relatively popular only within some small Russian Communities, who brought the music with them from their homeland.
In my personal opinion, Modern Talking exceeds the Girls in every aspect, including talent and ability. Their unfortunate temporary break-up disabled their plans for a US market. Still, many other talents arise, but all that we want to listen to is pre-pubescent boy and girl acts, along with hardcore rappers, who all seem to come form the ghetto. It seems to me that with the quantity of rappers, by now the Ghetto is richer than Beverly Hills!
Overall, the US music companies need to expand their markets and not control of what we listen to. Although the shelves of music stores are covered with CDs, try finding something like some Ace of Base stuff other than the three released albums, or even a Modern Talking CD.
