Short Term Liberalism

        It's now the end of June in the in 2003. Many feel that the numerous technological advances that the human race has made during the process of evolution toward its modern state will prevent history from repeating itself with events such as natural disasters, deadly diseases, and of course wars. However, the example of the past months of US and British presence in Afghanistan and most recent Iraq show that Afghanistan and most recent Iraq show that armed conflicts are now just a thing of the past, but will always be a part of the present.
        While some feel that America is going places where it is not wanted and should return to the practice of isolationism, others feel that US should intervene is world politics and military actions to protect the land of the free. Two interesting schools of thought to consider are that of policing or firefighting. When it comes to policing, officers drive around neighborhoods looking for crime that is going on, and sometimes preventing it from occurring simply because of their presence. On the other hand, firefighters only go to put out a fire after if started, without looking around for possible causes for fires (this is a general statement, firefighters take detailed steps toward preventing fires).
        The US currently seems to be practicing policing. Instead of waiting for tragic disasters to take place, troops are sent in to prevent horrific events from occurring. If looking closely, the past 20 years in the history of Iraq, a strong parallel to the history of the rise of Nazis in Germany is seen. In addition, Saddam claims his hero to be Joseph Stalin. He set up his government and country in the same fashion as the totalitarian ruler of the USSR had done 60 years ago. Thus, instead of waiting for WWIII, the US went in to stop Saddam from rising to the power level equivalent to that of Hitler and Stalin.
        Many don't see this point of view and simply feel that US is forcing its help on peoples that do not want its assistance. However, while at some points a bit extreme, America might be saving the entire planet from a nuclear holocaust or simple mass destruction and death. So next time you're choosing sides is a controversial subject, make sure to see all the sides of the situation before blindly jumping onto the short term point of view.
