What Is Love? Baby Don't Hurt Me...
By
Artem Kholodenko
The feeling or emotion of love can be defined in many ways: the butterflies in your stomach, the weak knees, the trembling body. But no matter how well one can phrase the verbal explanation of love, it will always be undermined.
Love is not something that should be described in word, but in actions. An individual might tell another that they love them, whether romantically or friendly, yet those words will mean nothing unless there are actions to back them up. One loved one will go to any lengths for another to make sure of the happiness of the individual. A person would sacrifice anything, just so the other is emotionally and physically complete.
Unfortunately, the society today has come to lower the status of love by throwing the word around a freely as they wish. The youth doesn't think about the consequences of their words, thus others get hurt. The most emotionally destructive of these "liars" are pretentious people. These love-it-alls seem to love everything and everyone - or so they say to you. To you, such folks might say that you are their best friend, while badmouthing someone else behind their back. Yet when you are gone and that other person is present, he assumes your role of the best friend. Small tip: what out for these people.
Love is hurtful the most in romantic relationships. The reason for that being is because when the couple is together and lovey-dovey, they say to each other how much they love one another. However, when things aren't as smooth, love gets the test of a lifetime and unfortunately sometimes fails. This is most likely in teen years, when peoples' thoughts, goals, and ideals are changing so rapidly that they might love the presence and personality of a certain person one minute and be totally repulsed the next. Yet life isn't fair and we just have to suck it up.
The love test is not only seen in love relationships, it is also witnessed in friendships. Some people might be your best friends when things are good and you can do something for them. But when you are no longer an asset, these folks pretend to not even know you or help you when you need assistance the most.
With the passing of time, one gains experience. This knowledge will help that person go through life and at some point be able to crack open a person without even getting to know them well. Regrettably, it takes a long time to gain this experience and great pains are felt along the way. One thing remains - hope.
