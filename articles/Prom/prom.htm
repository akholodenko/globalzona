Well, first of all I would like to apologize to all of you out there who had a bad or nightmare-like prom experience. There are more like you than you think, so start a club and show others how to avoid suffering for the rest of your life, especially during high school reunions.

However, the subject at hand is how to enjoy the experience while you still can, so let us get back to that. What I am about to tell you applies more to the senior prom, but feel free to apply the same principles to the junior prom as well.

Where to start? Wouldn't want to ruin the most important night of your life would you? First decide if you really want to go. Make sure that this is about four weeks before the big day. If the answer is not, go look at some jokes, if yes, continue reading.

Once decided that you do want to attend, time to pick the date. The number one choice of course would "her" or "him", but how? Well, the easiest way is to simply to come up and ask, even you girls! If you don't want to go in a romantic situation, a friend is never the wrong way to go. If that is your forte, going in a big friendly group is even better. The only bad way to go to the prom is "stag" or alone. The reason for that is because you will feel sad, especially during slow dances.

Got that date? Let's not stop here while on a roll. Now for the dinner plans. By now it is probably about three weeks until the prom. You need to find a romantic place, however, one that is not too pricey - remember, you are only about 17, 18, not Donald Trump. One the other hand, McDonald's is not an option. It is good to set a budget for the prom and make sure you stay in it in the process.

Once dinner plans are settled, off to dress/tuxedo shopping. For guys it's simple. Go to the local tuxedo shop, pick out what fits, looks good, and is comfortable and you're set. For girls it takes a little more time. It's good to pick out a dress that will in some way match the tuxedo and not sand out like a soar thumb. Go with some fancy, but not royal ball fancy; it might be too dressy. Also, make sure the dress is one you can sit down in, not just put on and stand. The purse and shoes are also not to be forgotten.

To continue on the out fit, the corsage is also important. Girls, a white boutonniere single rose will look great on a tux, and guys, a matching in color three-rose corsage will be fine as well.

Hopefully by now there are still about two weeks left until the big day. Time to figure out a ride. If you are still going in a big group, you can all chip in for limousine. It might be a little more, but it's okay for once in your life. If not a limo and over 18, you can rent a luxury car. While cheaper than a limo, you can still go in style. In a third option, you can drive your own, parent, or associate car. It will be completely fine, and save you a few bucks on your budget toward dinner or pictures. Finally, a forth option is to take a taxi. Still a good option, but not worry about parking.

Now everything is set for exception of tickets. Try to get them as soon as possible, because they may sell out. In the last days, double-check everything: date, outfit, dinner reservations, corsage / boutonniere, transportation, and tickets.

A few final tips: make sure that you have the tickets when you leave the house; have enough money for after things and pictures; if doing something after, bring a possible change of clothes; girls, consider a pair of sneakers for later, high heels are a killer.

That concludes the lesson on the prom. For additional questions or suggestions, email us at the address in our contact section or simply send mail to suggestions@globalzona.com.
