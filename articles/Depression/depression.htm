DEPRESSION 2000

        Depression. A powerful word. An undesirable emotion. However, it is something that no one can avoid. The feeling hits hard and fast, being triggered by various things, including a situation, a person, or a thought. For me, it is my current situation, which is really nothing to be depressed about.
        I don't know why, but lately I have been very depressed by a lot of the things that are going on around me. Practically nothing seems to be exciting for me. I get bored of everything pretty fast; all of the things have the feeling of "been there - done that". It is like I have out-lived my fun era. The last two years have been my true growing up period. Now, however, I feel like I have nowhere to grow.
        I need a new growth goal to reach. When I started high school I have a lot of people to look up to, like my older friends and all of those people. By now, they have all graduated and left to college. I will be there soon, yet for now I am stuck in the 12th grade. Some would say that I am an idiot for not enjoying my senior period, but I am not tied down by the grade I am in, I am more of universal person, and most of my "peeps" are sort of gone or will be soon.
        Sometimes I get the feeling that I am like my grandmother. In my head I recall a lot of things that I have done in the past, which now doesn't seem to be as big of a deal, yet still all good. However, I am not living for the day. I guess I need to start living for the day, but how can i when it's all played out. New things don't come around as much as they use to.
        Before, I would hear the word "party" and I would be the first to be getting people to go. The bigger problem would be getting my parents to let me to go, and I would be so excited about it all. Things sure have changed though. The word "party" doesn't come up even close to as often as it did before. Now it's all raves that are far away and with no way for me to get to. In addition, even teh simplest of activites like going out and hanging out at a coffee shop have gotten boring; they have been done simply too much.
