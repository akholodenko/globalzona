TEEN WEEKEND

        The last class is over. It is Friday. You rush off to get as far away from school as possible. But where are you running? Usually, you already have some sort of a plan - hang out with friends. Yet, what exactly are you going to do? No one knows. You call up a number of people and discover that you are as lost as they are. SO you tell them to give you a buzz if anything comes up.
        After covering a good five people with the same comments, you sit in front of the TV thinking of which option to take regarding the night's outing; what to wear and who's going to be there. An hour goes by - no calls. Two hours go by - you get a call from someone asking you what you have been asking for the last hour. Time flies while you wear out the cushion in your couch.
        Finally, you get a call. A friend is telling you that something might be up somewhere - very specific. You dress somehow and get picked up at some time. So now, a bunch of your friends gather in front of someone's house. You wonder where to go and what to do. A cell phone rings. It is another buddy on the phone wondering if the night is still young. Everyone hops into cars and drives off to pick him/her up. Now the party moves to that driveway. You gather, with an additional person now. You still can't think of what to do, yet hang around talking to your friends.
        A cell phone rings again - it's someone's parents. The person says they have to be home. Others mention a similar situation. So the highlight of the night becomes the point where you drop off people. Hopefully you get dropped off last, since you want to stay out later.
        You get home and call up a friends to discuss how stupid it was to no do anything and how next time you're not going to go unless there is a certain plan. Tomorrow you repeat the exercise.
        From this you learn that making a mistake once isn't enough, it's when you do the right thing, you remember.
