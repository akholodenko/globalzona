CALI

Biography
On December 14, 1979 a blonde haired, blue eyed baby girl joined her parents Dan and Cari in a hospital in the county of Orange. I am that little girl!!!! 20 years later I can look back and be proud of what I have accomplished in my life. I have two little brothers (though they are both bigger than me now) who have been there for me through everything and my parents, they have been so strong throughout my childhood. Believe it or not but I had my share and then some of giving parental headaches. From preschool till the 3rd grade I attended Calvary Christian School but over the summer of my 4th grade year my family moved to Riverside, CA where I attended Sonshine Christian. In the 6th grade my mother got the brilliant idea that she would take my two brothers and I out of school and homeschool us, this was the decision she ever made. At the time I hated it, going through middle school and high school at home was not a fun thing for a girl who thought she knew everything already!

I was an active little girl. I took ballet classes from as far back as I can remember till I was about 15, played softball for many years and basketball for a few years, that one didn’t really catch on in my life. In Jr. High I joined an all homeschooler marching band, I was part of the drill team where my brothers where involved in the drum chore. At the age of 13, my best friend Kim and I decided to enter the Miss Mira Loma beauty pageant that was being held in our city that Nov. We had a good time doing our thing and when it came time to announce the winners our heart were beating to fast for our little bodies to handle. I took home the runner-up prize (AKA Princess) and was obligated to attend other pageants, as “visiting royalty” That is where I was discovered.

A man by the name of “Tom” attempted to approach me but being my rude little self I told him “I already have an agent!” I took his card and gave it to my mom who, the next day, phoned him in regards to me. I met with him and 4 months later I was on a plane for Japan on a modeling contract, that’s where the fun began!

I traveled all over the world, Japan, Korea, Germany, Switzerland and Taiwan all before I was legal. I had the good times and the bad, it was nice to be independent but I also missed my family. I continued to homeschool throughout my travels and graduated in the Spring of 1997, one year earlier then scheduled.

For the year after high school I traveled, then I quit modeling all together and decided to go back to college. I got a job at Wells Fargo Bank where I lived a somewhat normal life.

This last December I got the crazy idea to get back into modeling while attending school to get my degree as a Pediatric Physical Therapist and Massage Therapist. That is where I am now! I love this job, as stressful as it gets it’s a great opportunity to speak out and help others.

My advice to anyone getting into modeling: Don’t do anything you don’t feel comfortable doing. Remember YOU are in control, NOBODY can make you do anything you don’t want to do! Keep reaching forward and never give up!
