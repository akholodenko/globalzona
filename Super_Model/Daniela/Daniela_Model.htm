Biography
I was born in Bucharest, Romania in 1979. I do not really remember very much about my country since I have lived in the USA for a while. I came here with my parents when I was 10 years old. I remember on the way here stopping in Italy for a week, and I had my first experience drinking Pepsi from a can. I had no idea how to open it, so my parents eventually used a can opener. My parents and I still laugh about it till this day.

We first moved to New Hampshire, where we lived for two years. I went to school there, but for the first year I did not know a word of English. I took a test to see what I knew for my basic skills and I aced that test, so the school skipped me a year. I took that that year to learn English, instead of going to the next level. By the time next year came around, I mastered the language pretty well. After two years in NH, my parents decided to see what California is like. We thought it was the greatest thing that there is; a state where it is always hot. Well, we quickly found out that it is not always hot in the San Francisco area where we moved. My parents fell in love with the place anyway, so we stayed. I went to Junior High that sane year where I met my best friend Vanna. After that I went to high school at Castro Valley High School. I was pretty dorky, I think...I did not have that many close friends; two or three at the most, and that includes my Drafting teacher Mr. Burns.

I was a little tomboyish, but I had my own style one would say. I met my first boyfriend, Josh, whom although I am no longer with, we are still great friends. Later on, I met my second boyfriend, Kevin, whom I am happily engaged to now. I got pretty good grades in high school, good enough to get into a university. I decided in my junior year in high school that I wanted to model. I had many people tell me that I am very pretty, but I did not think so; I was shy and I did not have very much confidence in myself. I went with my mom to John Casablancas, and I signed up for their classes. I learned some helpful things. They taught me some runway, some makeup tips, and some acting skills. Well, $1500 later, all I really got out of it was confidence. I am not saying that one should not take modeling classes, but I personally was not happy with what I got for the money. I was told by a JC representative that I was too short for modeling...this was after they took the money; before, when I first came, I was perfect for modeling. Well, I listened and did not pursue it any longer.

I went to school and focused on that, I got great grades, and was on my way to go to college when I realized that my parents were not be able to help me with anything for college. Both of my parents were working two full time jobs, but with so many bills, one being John Casablancas, it was hard. That is when I got a job at a mall for Guess, and although I tried my hardest, I was let go after the holiday season was over. I was so sad, and I was not sure what to do. That is when I decided that I was going to work for them again, only this time as a model. I went to a Junior College in the Bay Area, where I took classes to become a teacher. I want to work with young kids, elementary level. I really want to be the one that starts them off right; I want to make a difference in their lives. In the mean time, while attending school, I went to a casting call for a place called Style in the South San Francisco area. From them, I met my agency, Affinity, whom I have been with ever since. I have done many fashion shows, hair shows, promotions, and even two films as an extra. Last year, I decided that I want to learn HTML and do my own website. I bought a book, and started to teach myself. I eventually took a class on the subject, and I learned more techniques. I built my site, which will never be finished. I always learn new things about HTML, and I am always eager to try them out on my site.

In February 2000, I moved to the Los Angeles area with Kevin, my fiancée. I left my parents and Vanna back in the SF area. I miss them all everyday, but I still get to see them pretty often when they visit. My agency has also been keeping me pretty busy with some jobs here, and I travel back and forth from LA to SF often. My goals for the future are that I finish my education and become an Elementary School teacher. I have one more year at a Junior College, and then I will transfer to UC Los Angeles or UC Santa Barbara, have not decided yet. I love modeling and I will continue modeling for a long time hopefully. I have two reasons that I am modeling: one is to save up money for college, so that is what I am focusing on right now. My other reason is to save up money to buy my mother a home to retire in. She has been continually working at least one full time job, most of the time two full time jobs, and I really would love to see her happy, relaxed, and not worried about bills. She deserves this more than anyone out there; I love her so much. One of my modeling dreams is to do a print job for Guess or Bebe. I love both of their designs; every time I wear them I get comments that I should model for them...most of the time from people that I never met before. Ideally, I will get the opportunity to joggle between teaching and modeling.
